High-Frequency Bidirectional Resonant Converter for High Conversion Ratio and Variable Load Operation

This article proposes an HF/very HF (VHF) resonant converter structure in which the rectifier and the inverter switches can be driven with the same gate signal. The presented structure enables efficient bidirectional power delivery in the HF/VHF range for applications that require a large voltage conversion ratio. A 64-MHz, 12-W, 36-12-V prototype converter verifies the operation of the structure. This article further explores an interleaved architecture of the same circuit to improve efficiency and transient performance.

By L. Gu, K. Surakitbovorn, G. Zulauf, S. Chakraborty and J. Rivas-Davila in Journal IEEE JESTPE

August 16, 2019

Single-Turn Air-Core Coils for High-Frequency Inductive Wireless Power Transfer

The efficiency of megahertz-frequency inductive wireless power transfer designs is currently limited by the quality factor of the resonant tanks and the coils. In this article, we use two-dimensional finite-element analysis to optimize the cross section of single-turn air-core inductors for this application and demonstrate coils for wireless power at high-frequency and very-high-frequency industrial, scientific, and medical (ISM) bands. Our resonant tanks achieve diameter-normalized quality factors (Q d ) that are 25%-70% better than the state of the art for air-core coils at 6.78 MHz (Q d = 73 cm -1 ) and 13.56 MHz (102), and we show significant improvements at the 27.12 MHz (123) and 40.68 MHz (137) ISM bands.

By G. Zulauf and J. M. Rivas-Davila in Journal IEEE TPELS

July 31, 2019

On the Optimal Input Voltage of a Class-E Power Amplifier with GaN HEMTs at MHz Frequency Operation

We mathematically analyze the operation of a Class-E amplifier when facing with this Coss loss term. We have found that the conventional wisdom of using the highest input voltage to get the best efficiency no longer holds above a certain frequency. Furthermore, to allow this new loss term to be easily simulated, we propose a distributed loss model for the Coss loss based on a generalized Steinmetz equation (GSE).

By K. Surakitbovorn, L. Gu and J. Rivas-Davila in Conference COMPEL

July 25, 2019

Multiphase GaN Class-D Resonant Amplifier for High-Intensity Focused Ultrasound

Focused ultrasound treats tissue deep in the body without damaging surrounding structures. Driven by large-amplitude RF electrical signals, ultrasonic transducers can generate large pressure acoustic wave. Wide-bandgap semiconductors, e.g., Gallium Nitride (GaN), greatly simplify the design and reduce the requisite size and weight of RF amplifiers, and so improves the portability of focused ultrasound device. Here, we demonstrate a compact 8-phase 5 MHz Class-D resonant amplifier for a focused ultrasound cancer therapy application.

By L. Gu, Q. Stedman, M. Rasmussen, Ch. Pai, K. Brenner, B. Ma, A. Ergun, B. Khuri-Yakub, Juan Rivas Davila in Conference COMPEL

July 25, 2019

MRI Compatible DC Modulator for an Envelope Tracking Transmitter,

Typical MRI power amplifiers are characterized by the requirement for high linearity and a high peak-to-average power ratio. Here we present a MRI compatible DC modulator for envelope tracking applications based on a 4-phase interleaved buck converter using air core magnetics and utilizing a FPGA based digital controller. The modulator is capable of delivering 500 W into a 2.5 Î© load at an efficiency of 95.6%, has a 3 dB modulation bandwidth of 50 kHz, and has been tested with a Class-AB linear amplifier operating at 64 MHz to produce a modulated RF envelope with a peak power of 320 W.

By W. D. Braun, L. Gu, G. Scott and J. Rivas-Davila in Conference COMPEL

July 25, 2019
